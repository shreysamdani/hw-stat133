Rolling a die

The package "dieroller" is an implementation for simulating rolling a die a given number of times, and to visualize the relative frequencies in such a series of rolls.

Creating a die

First, create a fair die as follows

fair_die <- die()
fair_die
## object "die"
## 
##   side      prob
## 1    1 0.1666667
## 2    2 0.1666667
## 3    3 0.1666667
## 4    4 0.1666667
## 5    5 0.1666667
## 6    6 0.1666667

The probablities and outcome space can also be changed

# die with non-standard sides
weird_die <- die(sides = c('i', 'ii', 'iii', 'iv', 'v', 'vi'))
weird_die
## object "die"
## 
##   side      prob
## 1    i 0.1666667
## 2   ii 0.1666667
## 3  iii 0.1666667
## 4   iv 0.1666667
## 5    v 0.1666667
## 6   vi 0.1666667

# create a loaded die
loaded_die <- die(prob = c(0.075, 0.1, 0.125, 0.15, 0.20, 0.35))
loaded_die
## object "die"
## 
##   side  prob
## 1    1 0.075
## 2    2 0.100
## 3    3 0.125
## 4    4 0.150
## 5    5 0.200
## 6    6 0.350

Rolling a coin

Once you have defined a "die" object, you can use the function roll() to roll a coin a given number of times. The output will be an object of class "roll", which will contain the vector of rolls:

# roll 50 times
fair_50 <- roll(fair_die, times = 50)
fair_50
## object "roll"
## 
##  [1] 6 3 3 3 6 2 6 3 4 5 2 2 1 5 6 1 6 1 2 1 1 6 4 2 4 3 1 4 3 6 1 4 6 2 3
## [36] 1 6 6 4 5 6 1 4 2 5 6 2 3 5 6

summary() can be used to observe the frequencies:

summary(fair_50)
## summary "roll"
## 
##   side count prop
## 1    1     9 0.18
## 2    2     8 0.16
## 3    3     8 0.16
## 4    4     7 0.14
## 5    5     5 0.10
## 6    6    13 0.26

Plot of relative frequencies

You can use the function plot() to create a graohic with the relative frequencies of a series of die rolls:

plot(fair_50)
